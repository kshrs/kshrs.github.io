Why LinkedIn Is Fucked Up!

LinkedIn is no different from the other big tech social platforms.

There was a time when people walked building to building for interviews, waiting days for email replies. Now we scroll an app called LinkedIn — a platform once meant to connect with like-minded professionals and get job offers through casual chats.

In the beginning, the idea was clean. You’d post your accomplishments, list your qualifications, add some experience, and build a resume-like profile. It worked. People liked it. Recruiters used it, job seekers benefited. It grew fast - 10 million users in 4 years.

But what do I see now when I open LinkedIn?

Promotional content, viral posts, cheat sheets, random course notes, ads for certifications, and motivational quotes telling me to “work harder.”
Why do I need motivational quotes on a job platform?

Now it feels more like Facebook - post-driven, follower-hungry, with content made to grab attention, not help. I’ve seen people spend so much time watching and congratulating others, while doing nothing for themselves - yet they still feel like they've achieved something.

The human touch is lost, AI writes about their achievements and experience, collaboration has turned into content marketing, achievements and gratitude turned into likes.

LinkedIn was meant to be a place to share research, projects, achievements and experience. But now it is just a regular marketplace with free dopamine of information about others's success.

Piece of advice:
Use LinkedIn the way it was supposed to be.

LinkedIn was built for work. Now it is just another distraction.
